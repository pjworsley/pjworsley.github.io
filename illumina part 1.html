Illumina Sequencing Technology was a revolutionary sequencing technique of the early 2000s which allowed for much faster, cheaper and easier genome sequencing due increased read length and read speed. The technology involes 3 major steps: Library Preparation, Amplification and Sequencing by Synthesis.

The DNA fragments used are normally stored in vectors, where they can be maintained an replicated indefinitly. This is similar to the storarge of fragments for shotgun sequencing. The fragments cannot be worked on directly and must be prepared for use in the Illumina machine, the process of library preparation is as follows:

This process of adding adaptors to each fragment can be automated and is completed for every DNA fragment to create a library of prepared DNA fragments

The fragments are now ready for amplification; this involves attachment of each fragment to a flow cell. The flow cell is coated with DNA oligomers which are complementry to that of the adaptor sequences.

Washing the flow cell with the DNA the library of prepared DNA fragments will lead to each of them binding to the flow cell. Following synthesis of the complementry strand each fragments will now be bound to the flow cell in its own location. It is important to note that the adaptor sequences at each end of the fragemtns are different, this allows us to differentate which way around the strand is on the flow cell.
