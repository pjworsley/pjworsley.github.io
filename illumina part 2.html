Bridge Amplification begins with each fragment bound to the flow cell in a unique location and will allow us to build up a cluster of these fragments around this location. In this way we can amplify the signal of one individual fragment.

At this point we have two fragments next to each other. It is important to note that one of these strands is the forward strand and the other is the reverse strand. If we repeat the above process several times we will end up with 1000s of fragments around the same location.

Due to the unique nature of the each of the adaptors, we can remove the adaptors which bind just the reverse strand to the flow cell, leaving just the forward strand fragments in a cluster:

All the fragments in the cluster are now identical. This is process is carried out across the whole of the flow cell leading to thousands of identical fragments in thousands of clusters: This means we can move onto the next stage and sequences the strands by synthesis:
